## What worked

Three nights was the right length. The Edgewater paid for itself in waking up to the lake every morning, and Friday-night arrival made the most of Saturday. Pacing food across the weekend (one big meal per day, lighter stuff in between) kept it from turning into an eating-marathon.

The walks did a lot of the work. State Street end-to-end on Saturday, the lakeshore loop in the afternoon, the farmers' market Sunday morning — three different angles on the same compact city.

## What we'd change

- Less restaurant on Saturday. We triple-booked (small lunch, early dinner, late-late dinner) and the late one was a stretch we didn't need.
- Book a show in advance. Both the Overture and the Majestic were sold out — would have made Saturday evening more textured than "more food."
- Reserve a bike. The lake loop is genuinely fun on two wheels and the rentals were picked over by Saturday afternoon.

## Highlights

- Sunday morning at the Dane County Farmers' Market around the Capitol Square. The Capitol grounds are at their best on a quiet Sunday morning, and the market is the city showing off — cheese, brats, pastries, flowers, dozens of small Wisconsin producers.
- Tornado Steak House. An old-school Wisconsin supper club: relish tray, brandy old-fashioneds, a perfectly cooked filet, the kind of dim wood-paneled room you can't accidentally build. Worth the reservation, worth the second old-fashioned.
- Lakeshore Path from the Memorial Union out to Picnic Point. Three miles each way along Lake Mendota, mostly under big trees. Quiet, scenic, exactly the kind of walking-paced sightseeing this trip was about.
- Late-night music at the Crystal Corner Bar on Willy Street. A cozy room, a local band we didn't know, $5 cover. Felt like a small private discovery.
