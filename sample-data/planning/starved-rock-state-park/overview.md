Starved Rock is the rare Midwestern state park that earns the visit on its own merits, not as a consolation for not being a national park. The canyons are real canyons — narrow, mossy, cut sharply into the St. Peter sandstone — and the trail network is dense enough that two full days of hiking don't repeat. Spring brings actual waterfalls (snowmelt-fed); fall layers oak and hickory color over the canyon walls; winter freezes the falls into pale-blue formations that draw a small, dedicated photographer crowd.

The CCC-era lodge is the other half of the trip. Stone fireplaces, dark beams, an old indoor pool, a sit-down restaurant that's better than it has to be — it's the kind of building that justifies booking a weekend ahead just to lean into the lodge experience. Cabins are scattered through the woods behind the main building if the lodge rooms are gone.

Trail picks: French Canyon → St. Louis Canyon loop on day one (waterfalls if there's been recent rain), Council Overhang → Ottawa Canyon → Kaskaskia on day two for the deeper canyons. Add Wildcat Canyon if anyone still has legs.
