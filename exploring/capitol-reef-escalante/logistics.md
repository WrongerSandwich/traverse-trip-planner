# Logistics — Capitol Reef and Escalante Canyon Country

## Flights (MCI → SLC)

Delta operates nonstop MCI→SLC, ~3 flights/day, ~2h50m flight time. Southwest also flies the route (likely with a stop). United connects through Denver. **Delta nonstop is the cleanest option.** Book 2–3 months out; shoulder season fares have been seen in the $150–$350/person roundtrip range. Watch for sales.

## Rental Car

**Plan: mid-size or compact SUV (high-clearance).** This covers everything: all paved roads, Highway 12, Burr Trail switchbacks, Hole-in-the-Rock Road in dry conditions, and Cathedral Valley (with ford check). High-clearance SUVs run ~$50–80/day at SLC in shoulder season — book the category, not a specific model.

**Put the rental on a credit card with built-in collision coverage** to skip the CDW upcharge. At 4–6 days, this saves meaningful money.

## Seasonal Notes

**May** — Longer days, cooler mornings warming into 70s°F by afternoon. Fruita orchards in bloom. Lighter crowds than fall. Risk: late spring rainstorms can close Hole-in-the-Rock Road and Burr Trail dirt sections for days. Check BLM/NPS road condition hotlines before any dirt road.

**October** — Better light (lower sun angle, more contrast), fall foliage on Boulder Mountain, ripe fruit in orchards. Nights drop hard — freezing possible at elevation late October. More popular (fall color tourists); lodging fills faster. Capitol Reef hosts a Heritage Starfest in early-mid October — worth checking alignment.

**Both months:** Avoid the Scenic Drive between 10 AM and 2 PM if solitude matters. Arrive trailheads by 8–9 AM.

**Summer (June–August): Avoid.** 100°F+ in canyon bottoms. Flash flood risk spikes July–August. High crowds.

## Cell Service and Offline Maps

Cell coverage is limited to nonexistent through most of this trip. Verizon is better than T-Mobile in rural Utah, but even Verizon is spotty in Torrey and nonexistent in Capitol Reef, along Highway 12 between Boulder and Escalante, and on Hole-in-the-Rock Road.

**Before leaving SLC, download:**
- Google Maps offline for the region
- Gaia GPS (better for dirt roads and trails) — download the Capitol Reef and Grand Staircase layers
- NPS Capitol Reef app (free, offline maps included)
- AllTrails routes for specific hikes you plan

## Gotchas

- **Hole-in-the-Rock Road is brutal in a sedan.** Do not attempt after any rain, even the previous night. BLM Escalante Field Office road conditions: (435) 826-5600.
- **Cathedral Valley requires a Fremont River ford** — depth varies. Check with Capitol Reef visitor center before heading out: (435) 425-3791, option 4.
- **Rim Rock Inn closes late October.** Confirm they're open for your specific dates.
- **Hell's Backbone Grill is Thursday–Monday only.** Plan the Boulder overnight accordingly.
- **Gas is sparse.** Fill in Torrey before any major drive south. Limited fuel in Boulder (verify at Escalante Outfitters); reliable fuel in Escalante. Carry extra water on any dirt road.
- **Flash floods.** Do not enter narrow canyon bottoms (Grand Wash, Capitol Gorge, slot canyon approaches) if thunderstorms are in the area — storms 20 miles away drain into the same washes. Check weather before canyon walks.
- **America the Beautiful Pass** ($80/year) covers Capitol Reef entrance and Calf Creek day-use. Buy at SLC REI or the first park entry.
