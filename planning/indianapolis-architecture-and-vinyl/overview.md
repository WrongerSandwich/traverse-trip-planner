Indianapolis might be synonymous with motorsports, but this trip bypasses the speedway to explore the rich mid-century modernist legacy and vibrant cultural pulse of central Indiana. The anchor of the architectural pilgrimage is Columbus—a small town just 45 minutes south of Indy that punches miraculously above its weight, boasting masterworks by Eero Saarinen and I.M. Pei. Back in the city, Indianapolis' Broad Ripple neighborhood provides the perfect musical counterweight, featuring premier crate-digging at Indy CD & Vinyl and live sets at local mainstays like the newly outfitted Turntable and the historic Vogue. 

Evan and Erika's basecamp will be The Alexander in downtown Indy, a highly curated boutique property. While often tagged as a "literary hotel" due to its rich narrative elements and proximity to city culture, its true pedigree is an integrated world-class art collection curated by the Indianapolis Museum of Art. Flanked by sophisticated culinary heavyweights like Bluebeard and Milktooth in the neighboring Fletcher Place district, this itinerary is an immersion into Indiana’s finest design, sound, and flavor, leaning entirely into their taste for independent culture.
