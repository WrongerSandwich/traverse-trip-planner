## Day 1 — Saturday, May 2, 2026

**A Victorian river town frozen in amber: Earhart's birthplace, living aviation memorial, and an intact 1880s streetscape**

### Morning

- 9:00 AM — Depart Overland Park via I-435 north to US-73/K-7 north toward Leavenworth
- 9:35 AM — Leavenworth (optional 30-min stop) — C.W. Parker Carousel Museum, working antique carousels in the original factory; skip if maximizing time in Atchison
- 10:00 AM — Pick up K-7 north (Glacial Hills Scenic Byway / Amelia Earhart Memorial Highway) — wooded glacial ridgelines, creek crossings, river glimpses; ~45 min to Atchison
- 10:15 AM — Arrive Atchison (10:45 AM if Leavenworth stop taken)
- 10:15 AM — **Amelia Earhart Birthplace Museum**, 223 N. Terrace St. — 1861 Victorian Gothic cottage, period-restored rooms, house-tour format; 45–60 min. Admission: $12 adults / $10 seniors & military / $8 students. ⚠️ *Call Hangar Museum before departing home to confirm post-renovation access.*
- 11:30 AM — Drive to Atchison Municipal Airport, 16701 286th Rd (~5 min from downtown)
- 11:30 AM — **Amelia Earhart Hangar Museum** — Lockheed Electra 10-E (same model as disappearance aircraft), aviation history exhibits, VR flight experience ($5 extra); 45–60 min. Admission: $15 adults / $12 seniors & military.

### Afternoon

- 12:45 PM — **Paolucci Restaurant & Lounge**, 113 S. 3rd St. — family-owned, Italian-influenced American, full bar; walk-in. *(Fallback: Lopez de Mexico for lighter fare.)* ~45 min.
- 1:45 PM — **International Forest of Friendship**, 803 N. 14th St., above Lake Warnock — free, open daily; trees from all 50 states and 36 countries, each honoring a named aviation figure; granite path markers throughout. Created by the Ninety-Nines. Early May should be at or near full leaf. Allow 30–45 min.
- 2:45 PM — **Commercial Street Historic District** — 200–500 blocks; intact Italianate and Victorian commercial facades, former AT&SF Railway building, Masonic Temple block. Free walking tour map at visitatchison.com. ~75 min total.
  - **Muchnic Art Gallery**, 704 N. 4th St. — 1885 Victorian mansion; worth a look for the architecture
  - **Trinity Episcopal Church**, 300 S. 5th St. — Gothic Revival exterior and notable stained glass
- 4:00 PM — **Trotter's on the River**, 102 E. Atchison St. — riverside patio on the Missouri, bar food and drinks; 30 min if the mood is right
- 5:00 PM — Depart Atchison south via Glacial Hills Byway (or lasso return via US-59 south to Lawrence, then I-70 east — adds ~20 min, avoids retracing)
- 6:00 PM — Home, Overland Park
