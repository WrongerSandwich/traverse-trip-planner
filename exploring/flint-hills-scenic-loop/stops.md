# Stops — Flint Hills Tallgrass Prairie Loop

## Cottonwood Falls

**Chase County Courthouse** — 300 Pearl St. The 1873 French Renaissance courthouse is the anchor of the trip. Hand-cut Flint Hills limestone, mansard roof, and a three-story black walnut spiral staircase inside that's still in use. Self-guided tours weekdays 8am–5pm; call the Chamber at (620) 273-8469 to arrange a guided weekend tour. Free (donations accepted).

**Chase County Historical Society Museum** — Small local history museum; 30 minutes of context on the ranching and homesteading era. Verify hours before visiting.

**Schrumpf Hill Scenic Overlook** — 3 miles south on Hwy 177. Best formalized viewpoint on the byway.

**Grand Central Hotel & Grill** — 215 Broadway St. The primary dining destination and best lodging in town. Kansas-raised beef, serious steaks, full menu in a historic limestone building. Lunch Mon–Sat 11am–2:30pm; dinner 5–8pm. **Closed Sundays** — critical to know for a Sat–Sun overnight. Buffalo Bar on-site has more casual hours (10am–8pm). Reservations strongly recommended for dinner. (620) 273-6763.

**HeBrews CoffeeHouse** — 313 Broadway St. Main-street coffee; useful for a morning start before the Preserve.

**Doghouse Saloon** — 311 Broadway St. Local bar; solid afternoon beer, not a destination.

## Strong City

**Ad Astra Food & Drink** — On Hwy 177 near the Preserve. Gourmet burgers, specialty salads, Kansas-raised steaks. Proximity to the Preserve makes it a good post-hike lunch.

**Jacalito Family Restaurant** — 521 US-50. Mexican and American with full bar. Reliable backup.

**Chef Stan's Place / "The Little Restaurant on the Prairie"** — 225 Church St. Small independent diner; confirm current hours before counting on it.

## Tallgrass Prairie National Preserve

**Visitor Center and Historic Ranch** — Inside the 1881 Z-Bar/Spring Hill Ranch buildings. Free admission. Trails open 24/7 year-round; visitor center has seasonal hours (limited in winter — check nps.gov/tapr).

**Hiking** — Front-country loops from the ranch buildings are accessible for casual walkers. Backcountry routes through Windmill Pasture reach bison territory. Good walking shoes; no technical gear needed.

**Guided Bus Tour** — 6.4-mile backcountry tour, the most reliable way to reach bison territory and backcountry overlooks. Daily at 11am, last Saturday of April through last Sunday of October. ~90 minutes. Call (620) 273-8494 to confirm and reserve. No reservation needed for self-guided trail access.

**Bison Herd** — ~85 animals ranging in Windmill Pasture. Spring and fall are most reliable for active movement. Maintain distance — bison are unpredictable.

## Council Grove (en route)

**Hays House 1857** — 112 W Main St. Claimed oldest continuously operating restaurant west of the Mississippi. Worth a stop for the claim and the building alone.

**Flint Hills Books** — Independent bookstore; consistently above average for a town this size.

**Kaw Mission State Historic Site** — Free. Tells the story of the Kaw Nation's reservation period here (1840s–70s) and the Methodist mission.

## Lodging

**Grand Central Hotel** — 215 Broadway, Cottonwood Falls. 10 rooms, $175–190/night. Most central option; walkable to everything, on-site restaurant solves the dinner question. (620) 273-6763.

**Clover Cliff Ranch B&B** — 826A US-50, Elmdale (~8 miles from Cottonwood Falls). Restored 1860 Victorian limestone home on a 4,000-acre working bison ranch. Private bunkhouse and cabin options; breakfast included; horseback riding available. More immersive ranch experience; less walkable to town. clovercliffranch.com.

**The Lark Inn Guesthouses** — 421 Main St, Cottonwood Falls. Downtown guesthouse-style. (620) 273-1135.

**Spring Street B&B** — 371 Spring St, Cottonwood Falls. Check-in window is 3–6pm — restrictive; confirm before booking if arriving later. (316) 393-3194.
