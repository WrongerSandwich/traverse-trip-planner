# Route

Two routing options. The fast version is straight I-70. The good version uses the Santa Fe Trail corridor (US-24 / MO-41) for at least one direction. Recommended: I-70 outbound (get there efficiently), Santa Fe Trail backroads on the return.

## Outbound: Overland Park → Arrow Rock via I-70 (~2h 45m, ~175 mi)

- **OP → I-435 N → I-70 E.** Standard KC commute exit onto I-70 East at the Independence side.
- **I-70 East across western MO.** Pass Odessa, Concordia, Sweet Springs. Flat farmland, occasional service plazas. ~2 hours of straight interstate.
- **Exit 98 (MO-41 N) at Arrow Rock / Marshall.** 
The Town of Arrow Rock Missouri is located 13 miles north of I-70 on Hwy 41 in Saline County.

- **MO-41 N for ~13 mi.** Two-lane state highway through Saline County farmland. Rolling, scenic, low traffic. Last gas/services before Arrow Rock are at the I-70 exit — no gas station in Arrow Rock itself.
- **Arrive Arrow Rock village.** Park along Main Street or at the State Historic Site visitor center lot (signed off MO-41 just south of town).

## Return option A: Santa Fe Trail corridor (~3h 30m, ~190 mi, scenic)

This is the trip-defining drive. 
The trail parallels I-70 just to the north on MO 41/65/24 — a far more scenic way to go, exciting because for the most part you're driving on the original route and stopping at crossing sites, with ruts and old historic towns along the way. Following the National Park Service's Official Map and Guide, depart Arrow Rock towards Marshall.


- **Arrow Rock → Marshall via MO-41 S then W (~15 mi).** 
Marshall sits 15 miles west of Arrow Rock in the middle of rich farm country; you are traveling very near the trail as you drive, and when MO-41 turns into Road 240 you are on the actual Santa Fe Trail.

- **Optional detour: Weinreich Ruts north of Marshall.** 
Drive north on Road 41 (North Main Ave) crossing over the Salt River. The next left is County Road 275, and the Weinreich Ruts are just up ahead — remarkably well-preserved indentations.

- **Marshall → Waverly → Lexington via US-65 S to US-24 W.** Rolling Missouri River bluff country.
- **Lexington, MO.** 
Lexington embraces their Civil War history more than the SFT, and to this day there is a famous cannon ball lodged into the corner column of the courthouse, built in 1847 — the oldest courthouse in continuous use west of the Mississippi. The cannon ball is from the Battle of Lexington, fought in September of 1861.
 Worth a 30-min stop.
- **Lexington → Independence via US-24 W (~40 mi).** Continue along the south bank of the Missouri River.
- **Independence → Overland Park via I-470 / I-435.** Fight through KC traffic at the end (avoid Friday rush hour returns).

## Return option B: Straight I-70 W (~2h 45m)

If theatre runs late Saturday and you want a leisurely Sunday morning in the village before driving home, just retrace I-70.

## Vehicle notes

Default to **RAV4**. The Bolt is doable for this trip (175 mi one-way, well under EPA range), but Arrow Rock has no public charging, and lodging options in the village are old B&Bs unlikely to offer L2. If using the Bolt: charge to 100% before leaving, plan a top-up at Electrify America in Columbia (I-70 exit 124, 30 min from Arrow Rock) or at the Concordia/Sweet Springs Pilot stops which have EVgo. With a return via Lexington, charging gets thinner — stick with RAV4 unless you want to add a charging stop.
