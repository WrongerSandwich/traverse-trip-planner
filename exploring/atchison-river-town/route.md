# Route — Atchison Missouri River Town

## Option A: Direct (1 hour)

**I-435 north → US-73/K-7 north → Atchison (~55 miles)**

The fast route. Suburban highway north through Leavenworth County, nothing scenic, but efficient. Correct for a tight day trip where you want maximum time in Atchison itself.

## Option B: Glacial Hills Scenic Byway (~1.25 hrs, recommended)

**Overland Park → Leavenworth (~30 miles, ~35 min)**
Take I-435 north to US-73/K-7 north toward Leavenworth. Standard highway running through the metro fringe, fast but unremarkable.

**Leavenworth** (optional 30 min stop)
The historic downtown on Cherokee Street anticipates Atchison's character at smaller scale. The C.W. Parker Carousel Museum is a legitimate weird-museum candidate — working antique carousels in the factory that produced them starting in 1911. Fort Leavenworth's historic core is on federal grounds and gated, but a drive-through works. Skip if time is tight.

**Leavenworth → Atchison via K-7/K-92 (~35 miles, ~45 min)**
At Leavenworth, pick up K-7 north where it connects to the Glacial Hills Scenic Byway — a 63-mile NPS-designated Lewis and Clark Trail route running through rolling, wooded glacial hills with genuine topography. The road was also renamed the Amelia Earhart Memorial Highway on the stretch approaching Atchison. Real terrain: wooded ridgelines, creek crossings, river glimpses. This is the correct drive for a trip that values the journey.

## Return: Loop via Lawrence (optional)

Rather than retracing, consider returning via **US-59 south** from Atchison through McLouth and Perry to Lawrence, then **I-70 east** back to Overland Park. Adds maybe 20 minutes but makes it a lasso loop and positions you for dinner in Lawrence if the Atchison food situation doesn't satisfy.
