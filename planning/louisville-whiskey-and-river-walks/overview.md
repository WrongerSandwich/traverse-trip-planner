This trip trades the typical tourist-heavy bourbon trail for a deep dive into Louisville’s Victorian history, architectural legacy, and independent cultural venues. Driving east through Missouri and Illinois drops you straight into the Ohio River Valley, where Old Louisville stands as the largest contiguous collection of Victorian mansions in the United States. Rather than a manufactured heritage village, this is a living neighborhood anchored by Central Park, featuring beautifully preserved walking courts where late 19th-century residents used to promenade. It hits all your marks for historic and architectural interest without the usual crowds, offering an atmosphere that feels distinct from nearby regional destinations. 

Evenings on this route are designed to pair historic charm with contemporary art. By basing yourselves at the 21c Museum Hotel downtown, your lodging doubles as a massive, 24/7 contemporary art installation. You can spend the day admiring Richardsonian-Romanesque masonry in Old Louisville and the evening viewing modern video installations or catching a performance at the historic 1925 Brown Theatre. Adding in stops at Carmichael's Bookstore in the vibrant Highlands neighborhood and a classic, low-lit dinner at Buck's Restaurant in the historic Mayflower building ensures the itinerary leans heavily into local flavor, independent businesses, and culturally rich stops.
