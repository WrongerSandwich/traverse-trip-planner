## Reservations

Nothing requires advance booking for two people on a day trip. Paolucci is walk-in; both museums are walk-in.

## Seasonal Notes

**May 2nd context:** Early May is a fine window — temperatures should be comfortable for walking, and the Glacial Hills Byway will be at or near full leaf. Slightly earlier than the ideal late-May window but no meaningful difference in access or experience. Not yet summer-hot, no fall crowds.

## Gotchas

- **Birthplace Museum closed Sunday and Monday.** Saturday is the right call — confirmed open.
- **Hangar Museum runway renovation:** Airport runway work had a spring 2026 reopening anticipated; May 2nd is right at that edge. Museum vehicle access was reported unaffected, but call ahead Saturday morning before leaving to confirm full operations.
- **Paolucci hours:** A small independent — worth a quick check Saturday morning that lunch service is running normally.
- **Food scene is thin.** Paolucci is the only serious sit-down option. If it's unexpectedly closed, Lopez de Mexico is the fallback for lunch; Trotter's works for bar food.
- **Cell coverage:** Fine in Atchison proper; can be spotty on K-7 north of Leavenworth. Download offline maps before leaving.
- **Parking:** Free surface parking downtown and near the Birthplace Museum. Not a concern.
