Three nights in Madison turned out to be exactly the right length. Friday evening was the drive up plus dinner at Graze — small, busy, a few seats at the bar; the cheese board there is a fair statement of what Wisconsin can do. Saturday was State Street walked end-to-end with a long stop at the Capitol (the rotunda is open Saturdays, worth the detour), then the lakeshore loop in the afternoon. Sunday morning we caught the farmers' market at full tilt and grabbed early-afternoon coffee at Barriques before the drive south.

The Edgewater was the right call. Booking a lake-facing room felt indulgent on the Wednesday before, but every morning we ate breakfast watching boats come out of the marina and that was worth every dollar. The hotel restaurant is fine but skip it for dinner — the city's better food is a short walk away.

What we'd change: one less restaurant on Saturday (we triple-booked: a small lunch, an early dinner, and a late-late dinner, and the late one was a stretch). And next time, get tickets ahead for whatever's playing at the Overture or the Majestic — both were sold out the weekend we visited.
