# Route — Overland Park to Hermann, MO

Total distance: ~235 mi each way. Plan ~3.5–4 hrs driving with no stops; realistically 4.5–5 hrs Friday evening with traffic and a meal stop.

## Segment 1: Overland Park → Columbia (I-70 East) — ~125 mi, ~2 hrs

Standard I-70 slog. Get on I-470 or 435 east, merge onto I-70 near Independence, and settle in. Fuel/food in Columbia is the obvious stop (Booches, Shakespeare's Pizza if you've got time). **Active construction note:** 
Interstate 70 is Missouri's economic backbone, and the Improve I-70 project is a multi-billion-dollar initiative upgrading nearly 200 miles of this corridor — adding a third lane in both directions, modernizing pavement and bridges, with work divided into eight segments through 2029 and aiming for completion by 2030
. Expect lane shifts and reduced speeds in segments; check MoDOT before departure.

## Segment 2: Columbia → Jefferson City via US-63 South — ~32 mi, ~40 min

Exit I-70 at Columbia onto US-63 south. Easy four-lane drive through farmland to Jefferson City. Optional detour: stop at the **Missouri State Capitol** in Jeff City — 
the handsome neoclassical state capitol (daily, free), modeled on the U.S. Capitol and completed in 1917, is the central landmark; the rotunda and ground floor are packed with exhibits, and one-hour guided tours include a famous Thomas Hart Benton mural on the third-floor House Lounge walls
. Adds ~1 hr if you tour, ~20 min if you just walk in.

## Segment 3: Jefferson City → Hermann via Highway 100 East — ~50 mi, ~1 hr (the scenic part)

This is the segment worth taking your time on. Pick up MO-94 east briefly to cross the river, then MO-100 east — or take MO-94 the whole way (slightly slower but hugs the river closer). 
The stretch between Morrison and about 6-7 miles past Chamois (when you climb up the bluffs again) are on the Missouri River flats and can be vulnerable to flooding, so check before you go; the stretch between Morrison and Chamois is also almost completely flat
. The section approaching Hermann from the west climbs back up onto the bluffs with the best views.

Pull-off note: 
it's a great route for fall foliage — the only problem is there's no real places on the highway to pull over to take the best pictures, so rely on a helmet or dash cam
. Worth knowing if you want photo stops.

## Return options

- **Fastest (Sunday):** Reverse the route. ~3.5 hrs.
- **More scenic (slightly longer):** Continue east on Hwy 100 to Washington, MO (~30 min), pick up I-44 west, then I-70 west at Rolla. Adds ~45 min but lets you see more river-valley towns.

## Vehicle notes

**RAV4 (default):** No issues. Fuel up in Columbia or Jeff City; gas in Hermann itself is limited and pricier.

**Bolt (if used):** This is right at the edge of comfortable for the Bolt. ~235 mi each way exceeds 247 mi EPA range with any margin. Plan one DC fast-charge stop each way:
- **Columbia, MO** has multiple EVgo and Electrify America stations (Walmart on Conley Rd, Target on Stadium) — best mid-route option. Budget 45 min.
- **Hermann itself has minimal public DC fast charging** as of last verification — confirm on PlugShare before relying on it. Hermann Hill B&B advertises EV chargers for registered guests (
they have EV chargers for registered lodging guests at any of their three properties
) — Level 2 overnight is the play if staying there. Otherwise plan to arrive with enough charge to reach Columbia on the return.
- **Verify before trip:** PlugShare for any new DCFC in Hermann/Washington/Rolla corridor.
