## Old Louisville & The Walking Courts
The defining focus of the trip. Old Louisville has the largest collection of Victorian mansions in the US.
* **The Sights:** Do not miss Belgravia Court and St. James Court, which are grassy, pedestrian-only "walking courts" flanked by Chateauesque and Beaux-Arts homes. 
* **Tours:** Guided highly conversational 75-minute walking tours depart from the Historic Old Louisville Visitors Center (1340 S. 4th St., inside Central Park) Wed–Sat at 11:00 am and 1:30 pm. 
* **Admission:** $15 per adult. Waitlists are rare outside of massive city events, but walk-ins are welcome.

## 21c Museum Hotel Louisville
* **Lodging & Art:** Located at 700 West Main Street, this was the very first 21c property. It blends a ~90-room boutique hotel with a contemporary art museum. It is housed in renovated 19th-century bourbon and tobacco warehouses.
* **Museum Details:** The galleries are free, open to the public, and open 24/7 (video installations run 7 am to 1 am), meaning you can literally wander the exhibits in your downtime or after a night out.
* **Food:** The in-house restaurant, *Proof on Main*, is fantastic for bourbon tasting (120+ varieties) and local Ohio River Valley sourcing if you want an easy but high-quality meal on arrival.
* **Rates:** Typically ranges from $200–$300/night depending on the season and room type. 

## The Brown Theatre
* **Culture:** Located at 315 West Broadway, this opulent 1925 theatre hosts the Louisville Ballet, Kentucky Performing Arts events, and touring musicians. 
* **Tip:** The schedule rotates heavily. Cross-reference their calendar at booking. While you are on Broadway, swing by the nearby Brown Hotel to split an authentic, original "Hot Brown" sandwich before or after a show.

## Buck's Restaurant
* **Dinner Venue:** 425 W Ormsby Ave, nestled on the bottom floor of the historic Mayflower building right in Old Louisville. 
* **Vibe:** It hits the "historic interest" and "food worth driving to" requirements perfectly. It's an upscale but incredibly moody, old-school Continental restaurant heavily dotted with white flowers and mismatched china. White-tablecloth vibe but highly local.
* **Hours:** Dinner is served Mon–Sat starting at 5:00 pm (Closed Sundays). 

## Carmichael's Bookstore
* **Indie Scene:** 1295 Bardstown Road (Highlands neighborhood). Louisville's oldest independent bookstore. 
* **Vibe:** Hits the indie bookstore preference firmly. The Bardstown Road area hums with local life, independent coffee shops, and weird local haunts. 
* **Hours:** Mon-Thu 9 am–8 pm, Fri–Sat 9 am–9 pm, Sun 9 am–5 pm.
