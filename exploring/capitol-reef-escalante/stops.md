# Stops — Capitol Reef and Escalante Canyon Country

## Torrey

Primary base for the trip. Small, no chains, jaw-dropping evening light on surrounding cliffs.

**Lodging**
- **Rim Rock Inn** — First choice. Built 1964, motel-style rooms and cabins above the highway with views of red cliffs and Boulder Mountain. On-site Rim Rock Restaurant and Rim Rock Patio. Honest mid-century Western character. Closes late October — confirm dates if trip runs past third week of October. Book at therimrock.net.
- **Skyridge Inn** — B&B option on the edge of town, canyon views from every room, hot breakfasts included. More intimate; fills early for shoulder season weekends. Book at skyridgeinn.com.
- **Casitas at Capitol Reef** — Newer boutique, modern finishes (not historic character). Good backup if Rim Rock and Skyridge are full.

**Eating and Drinking**
- **Rim Rock Restaurant / Patio** — On-site at Rim Rock Inn; fine-ish Western dinner; patio handles casual meals and drinks.
- **Torrey Ciderworks** — Taproom serving ciders and meads made from local orchard fruit. Has a patio. One of the more distinctive small-town drinking spots in southern Utah. Confirm current hours.

## Capitol Reef National Park

- **Gifford Homestead** — 1908 farmhouse 1 mile south of visitor center on Scenic Drive. Open daily March 14–Oct 31, 8 AM–5 PM. Sells locally made pies, jams, ice cream. Get the pie.
- **Fruita Orchards** — NPS maintains ~3,000 fruit and nut trees on historic settler acreage. In season, eat fruit in orchards free; pick for a small fee when signed open. May: trees blooming. October: ripening fruit. Both are good.
- **Pioneer Register (Capitol Gorge)** — Names carved by 1880s wagon emigrants, still legible. 2-mile round-trip walk on flat sandy wash. Undervisited relative to how affecting it is.
- **Grand Wash Narrows** — 2.2-mile RT, no elevation gain. Canyon pinches dramatically. Best accessible canyon walk in the park.

## Boulder

32 miles south of Torrey on UT-12. Worth spending one night here to walk to dinner and make an early start on the Hogsback.

- **Hell's Backbone Grill and Farm** — The single best food in the region. Farm-to-table, menu shifts weekly around what's ripe from their six-acre organic farm. Open March–November, dinner Thursday–Monday only. **Reservations required via Tock (exploretock.com/hellsbackbonegrill)** — book as soon as trip dates are set. Fills weeks out for May/October weekends.
- **Boulder Mountain Lodge** — Operated by Hell's Backbone owners. Set in trees at edge of a bird sanctuary pond. Peaceful, characterful. Book directly; same lead time as the restaurant.
- **Anasazi State Park Museum** — Small state museum with excellent Ancestral Puebloan artifacts collection and excavated village site. Open daily. Worth an hour.

## Highway 12 Corridor

- **Calf Creek Falls (Grand Staircase-Escalante NM)** — 6-mile round-trip between Boulder and Escalante on UT-12. Trail follows canyon bottom to a 126-foot waterfall. Moderate, mostly flat with some sandy sections. Day-use fee (America the Beautiful pass applies). Get there by 8–9 AM in shoulder season.
- **Hogsback viewpoints** — Informal pullouts along the ridge section between Boulder and Escalante. Sheer drop-offs and canyon panoramas on both sides. Stop and look.

## Escalante

- **Escalante Outfitters** — Gear shop, cafe (pizza, simple meals), cabin lodging. The staff give accurate current conditions reports on Hole-in-the-Rock Road. Go here first if you're attempting that road.
- **The Loubird Inn** — Boutique motel, renovated rooms, local art, private patios. Consistent with the profile. Good base if spending a night in Escalante.
- **4th West Pub** — Bar in a 1940s service station. 30+ beers and cocktails, light food. Most characterful drinking option in town.
