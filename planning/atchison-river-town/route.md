# Route — Atchison Missouri River Town

**Date: Saturday, May 2, 2026**

## Glacial Hills Scenic Byway (recommended and default)

**Overland Park → Leavenworth (~30 miles, ~35 min)**
Take I-435 north to US-73/K-7 north toward Leavenworth. Standard highway through the metro fringe — fast, unremarkable, but it's over quickly.

**Leavenworth** (optional 30 min stop)
The historic downtown on Cherokee Street anticipates Atchison's character at smaller scale. The C.W. Parker Carousel Museum is a legitimate weird-museum candidate — working antique carousels in the factory that produced them starting in 1911. Skip if you want to maximize time in Atchison; it's not essential.

**Leavenworth → Atchison via K-7/K-92 (~35 miles, ~45 min)**
At Leavenworth, pick up K-7 north where it connects to the Glacial Hills Scenic Byway — a 63-mile NPS-designated Lewis and Clark Trail route running through rolling, wooded glacial hills with genuine topography. This stretch was also designated the Amelia Earhart Memorial Highway approaching Atchison. Real terrain: wooded ridgelines, creek crossings, river glimpses. In early May this section should be fully or nearly fully leafed out. This is the drive.

**Total driving time: ~1.25 hours from Overland Park, no stops.**

## Return

Retrace the Byway south. If you want a lasso loop, return via **US-59 south** from Atchison through McLouth and Perry to Lawrence, then **I-70 east** back to Overland Park — adds maybe 20 minutes and avoids retracing. Lawrence is a natural late-afternoon stop if the day ends with energy to spare, though Atchison should fill it cleanly on its own.

## If you need to skip the Byway

I-435 north to US-73/K-7 north direct to Atchison is about 55 miles and 1 hour flat. Only worth considering if you're running late or need to be home earlier than planned.
