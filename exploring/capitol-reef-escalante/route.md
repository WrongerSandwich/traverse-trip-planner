# Route — Capitol Reef and Escalante Canyon Country

## SLC to Torrey (~215 miles, 3.5 hours)

Standard route: I-15 south from SLC to Scipio (exit 188) → US-50 east through Salina → US-89 south to UT-24 junction → UT-24 east into Torrey past the park entrance. The San Pitch Mountains section on US-50 is quietly scenic ranch country; the transition on UT-24 starts paying off as canyon walls close in and the Waterpocket Fold comes into view west of the park. Allow 30–45 extra minutes if stopping in Salina — last reliable gas and food before Torrey.

**Alternative (adds ~1 hour, more scenery):** I-15 → US-6 southeast to Price → UT-10 south → I-70 west briefly → UT-72 at Fremont Junction → south through Loa to Torrey on UT-24. Passes through the San Rafael Swell — exceptional if you have daylight. Worth considering for one direction of the trip.

## Capitol Reef Scenic Drive (10 miles, paved)

Begins at the visitor center and runs south along the Waterpocket Fold. $20/vehicle fee (America the Beautiful pass honored; separate from park entrance). No timed entry required as of April 2026 — verify before travel, NPS has been expanding reservation systems. Passes Fruita orchards, Gifford Homestead, ends at Capitol Gorge. Arrive before 10 AM or after 3 PM to avoid midday crowd peak.

Key stops along the Scenic Drive:
- **Grand Wash** — Side canyon, 2.2-mile round-trip walk to narrows. Canyon pinches to 30 feet wide with 500-foot walls. Flat, no elevation gain. Best accessible canyon walk in the park.
- **Capitol Gorge / Pioneer Register** — 2-mile round-trip on flat sandy wash. Names and dates carved by 1880s emigrants, still legible. Undervisited.
- **Cassidy Arch Trail** — 3.4-mile out-and-back, 670 ft gain. More effort than the wash walks but views of the arch and Fold from above.

## Burr Trail Road

Runs 68 miles from Boulder east to Bullfrog at Lake Powell. **First 18 miles (Boulder to Capitol Reef boundary): paved, any vehicle.** This section includes the Burr Trail Switchbacks — a dramatic descent off the Waterpocket Fold into the basin below. Worth the detour from Torrey (~45 min each way via Boulder).

Inside the park boundary the road turns to graded dirt; a standard rental sedan can manage it in dry conditions. Do not attempt after rain.

## Highway 12 Scenic Byway (Torrey to Escalante, 62 miles)

The main artery of the trip. Drive south from Torrey on UT-12: climbs over Boulder Mountain through Dixie National Forest (ponderosa pines, views back toward Capitol Reef) → descends to Boulder → traverses the Hogsback (single-lane ridge, sheer drop-offs both sides, vertiginous) → descends into Escalante River canyon drainage → Escalante town.

Allow 2–3 hours if you stop. **Gas up in Torrey before departing.** Boulder has limited fuel availability; Escalante is the next reliable stop.

## Hole-in-the-Rock Road (Escalante south, dirt)

57 miles south from Escalante toward Lake Powell. **In a standard sedan: do not attempt.** Risk of high-centering on washboard. In a high-clearance rental SUV in dry conditions: first 26 miles are generally passable; Peek-a-Boo and Spooky slot canyon trailhead is at mile 24–26.

Check current conditions with BLM Escalante Field Office before any attempt: (435) 826-5600. Do not attempt after any recent rain.

## Cathedral Valley (remote north district)

500-foot sandstone monoliths (Temple of the Sun, Temple of the Moon) in the park's northern district. Requires a river ford of the Fremont River (depth varies; check with Capitol Reef visitor center at (435) 425-3791 option 4) and high-clearance vehicle. Skip in a standard sedan. Extraordinary if you can do it.
