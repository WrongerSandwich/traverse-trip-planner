# Stops

## Arrow Rock village & State Historic Site

National Historic Landmark village; the entire town is the attraction. Walkable in 2–3 hours; deeper engagement (museums + visitor center film + tavern meal) fills a half-day.

### Arrow Rock State Historic Site Visitor Center
- **Address:** 39521 Visitor Center Dr (off MO-41, ¼ mi south of village). Phone 660-837-3330.
- 
The visitor center opened in 1991 and features nearly 9,000 square feet of exhibit space. The center interprets the history of Arrow Rock and the central Missouri region historically known as "Boone's Lick Country." You can watch a 20-minute video "Arrow Rock: Frontier Town of the Boone's Lick" — an introduction and overview of the community.

- Exhibit topics include 
Native American presence focusing on Osage and Missouria tribes; the Louisiana Purchase and Lewis and Clark Expedition; the War of 1812 and clashes with American Indians from 1808 to 1815; town founding from 1815–1830; agriculture and the use of slaves on hemp and tobacco plantations; the Boone's Lick and the Santa Fe Trade from 1821 to 1848; and George Caleb Bingham — Missouri's most famous artist and Arrow Rock's most famous resident.

- Admission free. Hours vary seasonally — verify on Mo State Parks site before visit.
- Start here. Watch the orientation film, then walk into the village.

### Self-guided historic buildings (in the village)

The Black History Museum – Brown Lodge, Dr. John Sappington Museum, Shelby Log Cabin, the Old Courthouse, and the Print Shop Museum located on the first floor of the I.O.O.F. Lodge Hall are open daily for self-guided touring. Tram tours are available to select sites.
 Tram tours: 
contact The Friends of Arrow Rock Office at 660-837-3231.


Highlights:
- **J. Huston Tavern (1834)** — see dining below
- **George Caleb Bingham Home** — 
built by artist George Caleb Bingham, a historic house museum furnished as in the 1880s.

- **Old Courthouse, town doctor's home, stone jail** — 
all part of the walking tour.

- **Dr. John Sappington Museum** — local physician famous for quinine pills that 
saved countless travelers from malaria
 on the trail.
- **Black History Museum (Brown Lodge)** — interpretation of African American experience in Boone's Lick.

### Dining

#### J. Huston Tavern
- **305 N Main St.** Phone 660-837-3200.
- 
Yelp-listed hours: Mon–Thu closed, Fri 4:30–9pm, Sat 4:30–9pm, Sun 10am–2pm.
 **Verify directly** — these hours have shifted as the new operator (J. Huston Tavern Society) settles in.
- 
The Tavern is open before and after every Lyceum show, even on days they would normally be closed.

- 
"The oldest continuously operating restaurant west of the Mississippi has been serving travelers since 1834. Elevated comfort food including the Tavern's famous fried chicken, Chef Daniel's pork chops, steaks, and a full bar with specialty cocktails."

- 
Three dining rooms: the Sappington Parlor, the Huston Room and the Bingham Room. Drinks in the Tap Room (former mercantile).

- Worth knowing: 
the tavern unexpectedly closed in March 2024 and reopened later that year under a new partnership with the nonprofit J. Huston Tavern Society after no concessionaires applied to manage it.
 Operations are still maturing — call ahead.

#### Catalpa
- **302 Main St.** Phone 660-837-3324. catalparestaurant.com
- 
Hours: Mon–Thu closed, Fri 5–8pm, Sat 11am–8pm, Sun 11am–2pm.

- 
$$$, American New / American. Beer and wine. Outdoor seats. No reservations.

- 
Chef Liz recently rebooted the restaurant, going back to its origin of fine dining.
 
From the outside, Catalpa is no different than the historic facades around it, but once you swing open the antique doors, a modern culinary experience awaits — gold-leafed walls, live-edge counters and the restaurant's famous gold bathroom.

- This is the trip's "real food" stop. Plan dinner here Saturday if not doing the Tavern.

### Lodging in Arrow Rock village

Three small B&Bs, all walking distance to Main Street and the Lyceum.

#### Borgman's Bed & Breakfast
- 
Opened in 1982 in the National Historic Landmark village; visitors describe it as "comfortable and homey."

- 
Shared baths and double beds — "Bed & Breakfast in the home" as first envisioned.

- 
0.3 mi to Lyceum, 0.5 mi to State Historic Site, 0.1 mi to town center.

- Budget option, very personal experience with hostess Kathy Borgman.

#### Arrow Rock Station B&B
- 
Located in Historic Arrow Rock Missouri. One King Room with private bath and one Queen Room with 2 Queen Beds and private bath.

- Restaurant attached.

#### Down Over B&B (Prairie Park)
- 
A country retreat at one of three charming guest cottages on the grounds of the 1849 "Prairie Park" plantation, just 3-1/2 miles west of Arrow Rock on Highway TT.
 Phone 660-837-3268.

#### Vacation rentals
- Several Vrbo/Airbnb options in the village including a "Sweet Gum" cottage that gets strong reviews. 
Location is ideal — 1 to 3 blocks from any restaurant, coffee shop and theater.


## Boonville (alternate base, ~13 mi from Arrow Rock)

Larger town with more services. Useful as a backup if Arrow Rock B&Bs are full (likely on Lyceum weekends).

### Hotel Frederick
- **501 East High Street, Boonville.** hotelfrederick.com
- 
Built in 1905 by Charles Augustus Sombart, a local miller and banker; a noted local and state historical landmark in downtown Boonville.

- 
Hotel Frederick reopened in 2007 as a boutique hotel and today has 30 beautifully appointed guest rooms, a main level restaurant and bar, The Brick Room (lower level nightclub with live entertainment), and bike storage for Katy Trail.

- 
Convenient Katy Trail access, Missouri River views, dining at Hotel Frederick Restaurant + Lounge and weekend entertainment in The Brick Room.

- 
Check-in 4pm; sales tax 8.225% plus additional local lodging tax of 5%.

- 
Dogs under 70 lbs allowed; pet fee $40 per pet, limit two dogs.

- 
Live music in the Brick Room Friday and Saturday nights.


## Blackwater (alternate base, ~12 mi from Arrow Rock)


Blackwater was founded in 1887 alongside the Missouri Pacific Railroad line and became their only coal refilling stop between Kansas City and Jefferson City. The downtown commercial district is on the National Register of Historic Places.
 Tiny but charming brick Main Street.

### Iron Horse Hotel
- **101 Main St, Blackwater.** Phone 660-846-2011. ironhorsehotel.com
- 
Originally built as a boarding house in 1889, the building was renovated during a downtown revitalization period and opened as the Iron Horse Hotel in 2002.

- 
Historic boutique hotel offering small town hospitality with 9 unique guest rooms and multiple spaces for intimate weddings and events.

- 
Each of the nine rooms is themed after a famous railroad or route.

- 
Only 9 guest rooms, allowing for lots of personal attention.

- 
Pets allowed on request; charges may apply.

- Hot breakfast included.

## Side trips along the Santa Fe Trail (Marshall, Lexington)

If doing the scenic return:

### Lexington Battle of Lexington State Historic Site
- The 1861 battlefield with the cannonball-in-courthouse-column.
- 
Lexington embraces their Civil War history more than the SFT — there is a famous cannon ball lodged into the corner column of the courthouse, the oldest in continuous use west of the Mississippi.


### Fort Osage National Historic Landmark (near Independence)
- 
Highway 24 traces the south bank of the Missouri until you reach Fort Osage National Historic Landmark — wooden blockhouses surrounded by a log stockade, near Independence and Westport. Due to flooding at Franklin, the trailhead soon shifted toward these two towns.

- A natural last stop before reentering the KC metro.
