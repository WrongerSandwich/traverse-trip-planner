# Stops — Atchison Missouri River Town

**May 2nd is a Saturday — both Earhart attractions are open per their posted hours.**

## Amelia Earhart Birthplace Museum
**223 N. Terrace St.** The 1861 Victorian Gothic cottage where Earhart was born; period-restored rooms, house-tour format. Focused and well-sized (45–60 minutes). Open Tuesday–Saturday 10am–4pm (last entry 3:30pm). Admission: $12 adults, $10 seniors/military, $8 students.

Good first stop of the day — arrive close to 10am when it opens.

## Amelia Earhart Hangar Museum
**16701 286th Rd (Atchison Municipal Airport, ~5 min from downtown).** A Lockheed Electra 10-E — same model as Earhart's disappearance aircraft — plus aviation history exhibits and a VR flight experience ($5 extra). More conventional exhibit format than the Birthplace, good complement to it. Open Wednesday–Saturday 10am–5pm, Sunday 12pm–5pm (last entry 4pm). Admission: $15 adults, $12 seniors/military.

**Verify before leaving tomorrow.** Airport runway renovation had a spring 2026 reopening anticipated — May 2nd is right at that edge. Museum vehicle access was reported unaffected, but a quick call Saturday morning is worth it.

## International Forest of Friendship
**803 N. 14th St., above Lake Warnock.** Open daily, free. Trees from every US state and 36 countries, each planted in honor of a named aviation figure; granite path markers. Created by the Ninety-Nines (women pilots' organization Earhart co-founded). Strange and genuinely worth 30–45 minutes. Early May should have the trees at or near full leaf.

## Commercial Street Historic District
The intact 1870s–1880s streetscape. Self-guided walking tour map available free at visitatchison.com. Focus on the 200–500 blocks of Commercial Street: the former Atchison, Topeka & Santa Fe Railway building, Masonic Temple block, and the Italianate commercial facades. Compact enough to cover on foot in an hour.

**Muchnic Art Gallery** (704 N. 4th St.) — 1885 Victorian mansion as gallery. Worth a look for the architecture even if the current show doesn't interest you.

**Trinity Episcopal Church** (300 S. 5th St.) — Gothic Revival with notable stained glass; exterior worth a stop.

## Food and Drink

**Paolucci Restaurant & Lounge** (113 S. 3rd St.) — Lunch anchor. Family-owned for generations; Italian-influenced American menu with full bar. Opens at 6am, serves through the full day. This is the only serious independent restaurant option. Confirm Saturday lunch hours are normal before heading out — small independents sometimes adjust.

**Trotter's on the River** (102 E. Atchison St.) — Tavern and grill on the Missouri River with a patio. Bar-food quality, not a destination for cuisine, but the riverside setting makes it a natural mid-afternoon drink stop before heading home. Open Tuesday–Sunday.

**Lopez de Mexico** — Local Mexican; solid alternative if you want something lighter for lunch.
