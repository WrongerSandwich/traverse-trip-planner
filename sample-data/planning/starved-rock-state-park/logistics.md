# Logistics

## Lodging

**Starved Rock Lodge** (the only on-site option, and the reason to do this trip)
- Main lodge rooms: small, rustic, period-correct. Walls are thin.
- New wing: roomier, more motel-style. Less character but quieter.
- Cabins: scattered behind the main building, more privacy, mid-tier price.
- **Book 2–3 months ahead for fall and spring weekends.** Summer weekends fill earlier.

Backup if the lodge is full: Hampton Inn or Holiday Inn Express in nearby Peru/LaSalle. 15 minutes from the trailheads; loses the lodge feel entirely.

- [ ] Lodge room (target: 2026-10-17 / 18)

## What to pack

- Trail shoes with grip — the sandstone gets slick when wet
- Light rain shell (canyons hold moisture)
- Real water bottles — refill stations at the lodge, not on the trail
- Layers — canyons are 10° cooler than the bluff tops in shoulder season

## Anything tricky

- Trail closures: the park rotates closures after rain to protect eroding sections. Check the day before.
- Cell service is patchy in the canyons; download the AllTrails map offline.
- Eagle watching is a December–February draw if the trip slips to winter; bring binoculars and warmer layers.
