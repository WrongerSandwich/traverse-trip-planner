## The Alexander Hotel
**Indianapolis, IN**
Your home base. This Autograph Collection boutique hotel is an absolute gem designed for the aesthetically inclined. The hotel features an extensive art collection curated by the Indianapolis Museum of Art—highlighted by Nick Cave's spectacular *Soundsuit* and Sonya Clark’s portrait of Madam C.J. Walker constructed entirely of combs. 
- **Cost:** ~$170–$250/night, plus $46/night for self-parking with in/out privileges ($53 for valet). 
- **Vibe:** Highly curated, narrative-driven art space with refined, modern aesthetic minimalism.

## Miller House and Garden (Columbus, IN)
**Columbus, IN**
The crown jewel of Columbus's mid-century dominance. Designed in 1953 by Eero Saarinen, with striking geometric gardens by Dan Kiley and wildly colorful interiors by Alexander Girard, this is one of America's greatest examples of residential modernism. All tours start at the Columbus Area Visitors Center (506 5th St).
- **Hours/Dates:** Tours operate April–December (closed Jan/Feb). Tuesdays-Sundays most months; Fri-Sat only in March & Dec. 
- **Admission:** $30 per person for a 90-minute guided tour. 
- **Note:** Children under 10 are strictly barred. No interior photos beyond a single designated spot. Restrooms are *only* available at the Visitor Center, not the house itself. 

## Indy CD & Vinyl 
**Broad Ripple (806 Broad Ripple Ave)**
The premier independent record shop in central Indiana and a vibrant hub for the local music scene. With deep selections of new and used LPs across genres, it’s exactly the crate-digging environment you're looking for. 
- **Hours:** Monday-Saturday 11am–7pm; Sunday 11am–6pm. 
- **Vibe:** Authentically indie with a knowledgeable staff; heavily involved in Record Store Day.

## Turntable & The Vogue
**Broad Ripple (6281 N College Ave)**
The Vogue is Indy's iconic historic music hall, but adjacent to it is *Turntable*, Forty5's newest 18+ venue. Turntable functions as a vinyl listening room and speakeasy up front with an expansive live music space in the back.
- **Tips:** Check both venue schedules prior to the trip to snag tickets for local or touring indie bands.

## Bluebeard
**Fletcher Place (653 Virginia Ave)**
Located literal minutes from The Alexander, Bluebeard is a cornerstone of Indy's sophisticated food scene. Headed by Executive Chef Alan Sternberg, the menu is contemporary Midwestern in the best way imaginable. 
- **Vibe:** Upscale but unpretentious, utilizing locally sourced ingredients (expect incredible handmade pastas—like their rigatoni with ssamjang walnut pesto—and rich pork dishes).
- **Reservations:** They accept *limited* reservations but always leave ample room for walk-ins. 

## Milktooth
**Fletcher Place (534 Virginia Ave)**
One of the most talked-about dining spots in the Midwest, this converted garage is a brunch mecca. Ingredients lean highly imaginative, with standouts like the sourdough pearl sugar waffle and savory Dutch baby pancakes.
- **Vibe:** Very trendy, chef-driven breakfast. 
- **Note:** Prepare for a wait. The kitchen is notorious for a "no substitutions" policy; surrender to the chef's vision and you'll have an incredible meal.
