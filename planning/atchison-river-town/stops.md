# Stops — Atchison Missouri River Town

## Amelia Earhart Birthplace Museum
**223 N. Terrace St.** The 1861 Victorian Gothic cottage where Earhart was born; period-restored rooms, house-tour format. Focused and well-sized (45–60 minutes). Open Tuesday–Saturday 10am–4pm (last entry 3:30pm); closed Sunday and Monday. Admission: $12 adults, $10 seniors/military, $8 students.

**Critical: closed Sunday and Monday.** Plan your visit for Saturday if running a weekend trip.

## Amelia Earhart Hangar Museum
**16701 286th Rd (Atchison Municipal Airport, ~5 min from downtown).** A Lockheed Electra 10-E — same model as Earhart's disappearance aircraft — plus aviation history exhibits and a VR flight experience ($5 extra). More conventional exhibit format than the Birthplace, good complement to it. Open Wednesday–Saturday 10am–5pm, Sunday 12pm–5pm (last entry 4pm); closed Monday–Tuesday. Admission: $15 adults, $12 seniors/military.

**Airport runway was under renovation with spring 2026 reopening anticipated.** Museum road access was unaffected, but verify current operating status before visiting.

## International Forest of Friendship
**803 N. 14th St., above Lake Warnock.** Open daily, free. Trees from every US state and 36 countries, each planted in honor of a named aviation figure; granite path markers. Created by the Ninety-Nines (women pilots' organization Earhart co-founded). Strange and genuinely worth the 30–45 minutes. Best in spring (May) when leafed out.

## Commercial Street Historic District
The intact 1870s–1880s streetscape is the architectural core of the trip. Self-guided walking tour map available free at visitatchison.com. Focus on the 200–500 blocks of Commercial Street: the former Atchison, Topeka & Santa Fe Railway building, Masonic Temple block, and the Italianate commercial facades. Compact enough to cover on foot in an hour.

**Muchnic Art Gallery** (704 N. 4th St.) — 1885 Victorian mansion as gallery. Worth a look for the architecture even if the current show doesn't interest you.

**Trinity Episcopal Church** (300 S. 5th St.) — Gothic Revival with notable stained glass; exterior worth a stop.

## Food and Drink

**Paolucci Restaurant & Lounge** (113 S. 3rd St.) — The dinner pick. Family-owned for generations; Italian-influenced American menu with full bar. Opens at 6am for breakfast, serves through dinner. This is the only serious independent restaurant option; plan around its hours. Kitchen closes ~9pm most nights. Verify current hours before relying on it.

**Trotter's on the River** (102 E. Atchison St.) — Tavern and grill on the Missouri River with a patio. Bar-food quality, not a destination for cuisine, but the riverside setting makes it a natural late-afternoon drink stop. Open Tuesday–Sunday.

**Lopez de Mexico** — Local Mexican; solid lunch option with good community reviews.

**Return via Lawrence** — If food is a priority, the loop return via US-59 positions you for dinner in Lawrence (Sunflower Bike Club, Merchants Pub, Free State Brewing) which resolves the thin-food-scene problem entirely.

## Lodging

**Tuck U Inn at Glick Mansion** (503 N. 2nd St.) — The correct lodging choice. Historic 1870s mansion (home of Governor Glick), five-bedroom B&B, private baths, period furnishings, full breakfast included. Walking distance from the Birthplace Museum. 5.0 rating across 168 Tripadvisor reviews. Five rooms means it fills for weekend stays; book early. Rates typical for comparable B&Bs in the region: ~$150–200/night.

**Quality Inn Atchison** — Chain fallback if the Mansion is full. Includes breakfast and indoor pool. Works functionally but misses the point of this trip.
