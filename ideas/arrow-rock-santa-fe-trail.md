pitch: Arrow Rock is a National Historic Landmark village on the Missouri River bluffs that has barely changed since the 1840s — the 1872 tavern still operates, the stone buildings along the main street are intact, and the Lyceum Theatre (oldest continuously operating regional theater west of the Mississippi) runs productions through summer and fall. It was a major outfitting point on the Santa Fe Trail, which you can trace along the river road approaching from the west. About 2 hours east on I-70 then north.
