# Stops — Hermann, MO and surroundings

## Downtown Hermann (walkable historic district)

The compact grid of 19th-century brick — Schiller Street, Market Street, 1st through 4th — is where you'll spend most of your time. Lots of murals, shops, and tasting rooms within a few blocks. 
Hermann is full of historical culture, making it fun to explore each street and even the alleys; while exploring, many visitors enjoy the collection of murals sprawled across town — Hermann has lots of murals, both indoor and outdoor, each displaying the town's history
. The Visit Hermann self-guided mural walking tour is free and a good way to orient.

**Visitor Center:** 
Hermann Visitor Center at 301 Wharf Street (at the Amtrak Station) is open from 8 AM to 8 PM with public restrooms available
 — good first stop for current event flyers and a paper map.

## Wineries

Hermann hours change seasonally and many places run reduced schedules midweek — 
Hermann is a small charming town and some businesses focus all their energy on a few days of the week; before planning your trip, check operating hours of tasting rooms, shops, and restaurants you're hoping to visit
. Always confirm 24–48 hours before.

### Stone Hill Winery
1110 Stone Hill Hwy, on the bluff just southwest of downtown. 
Founded in 1847 by German immigrants, Stone Hill Winery found international acclaim with its Norton declared "Best Wine of All Nations." Cut down in its prime by Prohibition, the winery sat dormant for 45 years until 1965, when Jim and Betty Held — two small farmers with less than $1200 in their bank account — decided to take a chance and restore the winery
. The flagship: hilltop, biggest operation, best cellar tour in town. Tasting includes a stemless wine glass to keep. Reviews favor the Traminette and Norton.

### Adam Puchta Winery
1947 Frene Creek Rd, ~2.5 miles west of downtown via Hwy 100. 
Established in 1855 — the oldest continuously owned and operated family farmed winery in the United States, located just 2.5 miles west of Hermann off Route 100, nestled beside Frene Creek
. The local-favorite quieter pick. 
Summer hours (April–October): Mon–Thu 10 am–5 pm, Fri & Sat 10 am–6 pm, Sun 11 am–5 pm; winter hours (Nov–March): Mon–Sat 10 am–5 pm, Sun 11 am–5 pm
. 
Tastings are $10 for 6 wines, and you get to keep the glass
. The on-site **1855 Cellar Bistro** is open Wed–Fri 11 am–4 pm, Sat 11 am–5 pm. Reviewer consensus: more relaxed vibe than Stone Hill, friendly staff, resident cats.

### Hermannhof Winery
Downtown on East 1st St — walkable from anywhere in the historic district. 
Hermannhof's ten magnificent stone cellars and charming brick superstructure are among the 100 early Hermann buildings on the National Register; construction commenced in 1848 and was completed in 1852 in the French section of Hermann, and Hermannhof's wine making was initially a small part of this very successful brewery
. Best historic-cellar atmosphere right in town. Adjacent: 
the Tin Mill Brewery, located in a historic grain processing plant, for German style beer and food; or for wine, the main winery Hermannhof, with their California tasting room Dierberg and Star Lane right next door
 — the Three California Vineyards Tasting Room is a quirky bonus if you want a side-by-side with West Coast bottles.

### OakGlenn Vineyards & Winery
On the bluffs east of town. Reviewer-favorite for views over the river valley; less crowded than the in-town spots but a short drive.

### Other wine trail members worth knowing

The Hermann Wine Trail features 7 local family-owned Missouri wineries and vineyards
 — including Bias, Robller (in New Haven, ~20 min east), and Endless Summer. If you've only got one day, two in-town stops + Adam Puchta is a complete circuit.

## Breweries & distilleries

### Tin Mill Brewery
1110 Stone Hill Hwy. 
Located in a historic grain processing plant, Tin Mill Brewery features authentic German brewing — take a self-guided tour to see their copper brewing kettles imported from the Hallertau region of Germany. They brew most of their beers according to the German Purity Law of 1516, and offer tastings, a restaurant, gift shop, and live music
. 
Hours: Mon, Thu–Sat 11:30 AM–7 PM; Sun 11:30 AM–3:30 PM
 (verify; closed Tue/Wed historically).

### Other distilleries

Black Shire Distillery and Fernweh Distilling Co., with 2 more distilleries to soon open
. Copper Mule and Lionheart Whiskey Co. also operate downtown. Juniper's Gin Library gets good reviews for cocktails.

## Museums & history

### Deutschheim State Historic Site
Corner of E 1st & Schiller. 
The Deutschheim State Historic Site offers a glimpse of what life was like for German immigrants during the mid 19th century. The buildings were built in the 1840s and 1850s, full of tools and artifacts used more than a century ago. During a guided tour you'll see some of the oldest surviving buildings in Hermann, authentic furnishings, and one of Hermann's original vineyards
. Verify tour times day-of (state historic sites often have limited weekend tour windows).

### Historic Hermann Museum
4th & Schiller Streets, in the old German School building. 
The museum was inexpensive and had a wealth of historical information and exhibits — tools, medical, clothing, toys, rooms, wars, wine, family history. Well worth 1 to 2 hours
.

### Hermann Farm

Experience what life was like for farmers in the 1800s by taking a tour of Hermann Farm — a two-hour tour in a covered tram, learning about the farm's history and some of Hermann's most notable founders
. Two hours is a real commitment; skip if you're more interested in town/wineries.

### City of Hermann Caboose Museum
Free, small, by the Amtrak station. 
Visitors can see interactive displays and exhibits telling the story of railroad history in Missouri and how railroads led to the westward expansion of the United States
. 15-minute stop.

## Riverfront Park

Walk to the river, see the Lewis & Clark marker. Right next to Amtrak/Visitor Center. Good sunset spot.

## Food

### Hermann Wurst Haus
234 E 1st St. The headline German lunch. 
Missouri Wine Country's premier meat and sausage shop featuring more than 65 varieties of award-winning bratwurst and German sausage as well as Grand Champion house-smoked bacons; all sausages handcrafted by three-time Hall of Fame owner Mike Sloan, who has won more than 600 international, national, state, and regional awards including 16 Best of Show honors and 29 international awards from the German Butcher's Association in Frankfurt
. 
Made-to-order breakfast Mon–Fri 5:30–10:30 am; All You Can Eat Wurst Breakfast Sat & Sun 8–11 am, including Grand Champion Bacon, sausage or ham, scrambled eggs, biscuits and gravy, cheesy hash browns, and cinnamon buns
. Caveat: 
one Google reviewer noted two sausage platters served on Styrofoam plates with plastic silverware set them back $93
 — it's deli-style, not white-tablecloth, despite the prices.

### Vintage 1847 Restaurant (at Stone Hill)

Just steps from the main winery, Vintage 1847 Restaurant provides contemporary American cuisine, along with German house specialties that pair perfectly with Stone Hill's award-winning wines
. 
The restaurant is located within Stone Hill Winery's original carriage house and barn built in 1847 — contemporary American with German house specialties, and no visit to Stone Hill or Hermann for that matter is complete without sitting down to eat here
. Dinner reservations strongly recommended weekends.

### Tin Mill Restaurant / Concert Hall and Barrel Bar / Sugar Momma's

Discover seafood spaetzle and other German delights at the Tin Mill Restaurant, Kartoffelpuffer (potato pancakes) and Sauerbraten at Vintage Restaurant, or unique offerings like the Reuben Quesadilla at Willie's Phillys; enjoy a Germantown Burger or other German specialties at Concert Hall and Barrel Bar; for dessert, check out Sugar Momma's for Bavarian Strudel, Apfel Dumplings, or German treats
.

### Yelp's German shortlist for the area

Best German in Hermann: Vintage Restaurant, Das Steinhaus, Tin Mill Brewing Company, Ursula's Schnitzelhaus, Dreamland Palace German Restaurant, The Hub Restaurant & Lounge, Wittmond Hotel Restaurant
 — note some are outside Hermann proper (Dreamland Palace is in nearby Foristell area).

## Lodging — what to actually book

Hermann is heavy on B&Bs and vacation rentals; chain hotels are minimal. Book early — rooms go fast for any festival weekend.

### Old Vine Bed & Breakfast

The award-winning Old Vine Bed & Breakfast offers lodging perfectly nestled within walking distance of Hermann's wineries, restaurants, distilleries, charming boutiques, and the Amtrak station; every morning begins with a delicious farm-to-table breakfast, thoughtfully prepared as part of your stay, using locally sourced ingredients whenever possible
. Strong reviews, walkable, characterful. First pick if available.

### The Rafters B&B

In the Historic District at the corner of 3rd and Schiller, just steps from dining, museums, trolley stops, antique shops; the building is OLD — built in 1869 — but isn't filled with stodgy old antiques. Modern, comfortable furniture mixed with a few pieces with character, all designed with guest comfort in mind
. 
The ground floor features a cigar/pipe smoking lounge that also serves top shelf libations
.

### Hermann Hill Vineyard Inn & Spa

Upscale boutique B&B with 20 premium suites & cottages — luxurious amenities include steam showers with rainforest shower heads, Sanijet jetted tubs, fireplaces, flat-screen TVs with Blu-ray, and king-size beds
. The splurge option, on a hill overlooking vineyards. 
EV chargers for registered lodging guests at any of their three properties
 — useful if Bolting it.

### Captain Wohlt Inn

A perfect place for everything from a romantic getaway to a family get-together, with four wonderful buildings nestled in the heart of Hermann's historic district
. Mid-priced, well-located.

### Other to consider
The Inn at Hermannhof, Harbor Haus Inn & Suites, Stone Haus B&B, Abigail's Grape Leaf, The Bohlken House. 
The Artists Suites is a boutique hotel located above the Blank Canvas Gallery and Studio, with each room decorated for a renowned artist — choose the Da Vinci or Claude Monet suite
 — quirky, in the spirit of the trip profile.

## The Hermann Trolley

The Hermann Trolley is one of the best ways to experience the heart and soul of Missouri's wine country — through it you can visit Hermann's wineries and distilleries
. Hop-on/hop-off; the right move if you want to taste at multiple wineries without driving. Verify schedule and pricing day-of.
