## Seasonal Notes

**Late April–May (best):** New green growth, bison calves, wildflowers on Hwy 177. Bus tour season opens last Saturday of April. Sweet spot for landscape color and bison activity.

**September–October (second best):** Tallgrass turns bronze and amber — the prairie's equivalent of fall color, peaking mid-October. Comfortable temperatures.

**AVOID late March–mid-April (burn season):** Flint Hills ranchers burn roughly 2.2 million acres annually to maintain prairie health, with the heavy window in late March through mid-April. During active burns: Hwy 177 has charred-black fields instead of green prairie, smoke haze is common, and air quality advisories can affect Chase County. If April is the target, aim for the last weekend of April or later, after green-up has started on burned pastures.

**Summer (June–August):** Big bluestem peaks in late June–July (can reach 6 feet) but heat and humidity are significant — 90°F+ days are common. Early morning is the only comfortable outdoor window.

**Winter:** Trails open 24/7 but visitor center has reduced hours and the bus tour doesn't run. Dormant prairie; not recommended for a first visit.

## Cell Coverage

Adequate on major carriers (AT&T and Verizon best for rural Kansas) in Cottonwood Falls and Strong City. Leave town on the byway or into the Preserve backcountry and service becomes unreliable. Download offline maps before leaving home — Google Maps offline or Gaia GPS. The bus tour's backcountry route will likely have no signal.

## EV Note

No DC fast chargers in Cottonwood Falls or Strong City. Not relevant for the RAV4, but if the Bolt is ever considered for this trip, plan charging carefully — nearest fast charger is likely Emporia or I-35.

## Pet Sitter

Even a one-nighter requires Terra to check on Goose and Bean. The trip's 1–2 night flexibility means the sitter window could be one evening to two nights — confirm availability before booking lodging or locking dates. This is a real constraint, not just a formality.

## Gotchas

- **Burn season** — The single most important planning factor. Late March–mid-April is the wrong time for this trip.
- **Grand Central is closed Sundays** — If doing a Sat–Sun overnight, Sunday morning means no Grand Central. Have a backup for Saturday lunch in case that's when you need it.
- **Preserve visitor center has limited winter hours** — Trails are always open, but the house tours and bookstore have seasonal hours. Check nps.gov/tapr before visiting outside April–October.
- **No camping at the Preserve** — Overnight vehicle parking is prohibited. All lodging must be off-site in Cottonwood Falls or Strong City.
- **Matfield Green has no services** — Gas up in Cottonwood Falls or Strong City before heading south on 177.
- **Spring Street B&B check-in is 3–6pm only** — Restrictive. Confirm if arriving outside that window.
