Highway 177 through the Flint Hills is the only stretch of road from Kansas City that earns the phrase "the drive is the trip." Most of the Great Plains was broken by the plow within a generation of settlement, but the Flint Hills escaped because its thin limestone soil couldn't hold a steel blade. What remains is the largest surviving expanse of tallgrass prairie in North America — a rolling, treeless terrain that looks almost oceanic in strong wind, with big bluestem and indiangrass topping out above head height by late summer. The visual effect on Hwy 177 between Council Grove and Cottonwood Falls is unlike anything else within two hours of Kansas City.

Cottonwood Falls itself is small — under 1,000 people — and organized around a single genuinely distinguished anchor: the Chase County Courthouse, built in 1873 from hand-cut Flint Hills limestone in French Renaissance style, with a mansard roof and a three-story black walnut spiral staircase inside. It still functions as an active courthouse. The surrounding main street on Broadway is honest rather than over-curated: a coffee shop, a saloon, a hotel with a serious kitchen, and not much else trying too hard. This is not a place that has been rediscovered and polished.

The Tallgrass Prairie National Preserve, headquartered at the historic Z-Bar/Spring Hill Ranch a few miles north of Cottonwood Falls near Strong City, adds the dimension that turns this from a drive-and-look trip into a walk-around trip. The preserve's 40-plus miles of trails are mostly mowed-grass paths and old ranch roads — honest walking, not technical hiking — and the bison herd (roughly 85 animals) ranges through the Windmill Pasture. A guided bus tour runs daily from late April through October and is the most reliable way to see the backcountry.

The standard one-night pattern: drive down Friday afternoon via Hwy 177, walk the Preserve Saturday morning, lunch and courthouse in Cottonwood Falls, dinner at Grand Central, home Sunday morning. A second night adds Council Grove on the way or a longer Preserve loop.
