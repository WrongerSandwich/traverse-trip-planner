# Route — Overland Park to Madison

Total: ~489 miles, ~7h 20m driving, no major mountain passes. 
The total driving time is 7 hours, 20 minutes
 from Kansas City to Madison. This is at the outer edge of the "long weekend" radius — Thursday evening departure or a very early Friday start is the practical move.

Use the **RAV4** for this trip. 489mi each way, with the Iowa stretch running through small towns with limited DC fast-charging that's reliable for the Bolt's ~55kW max. Not worth the planning friction.

## Segment 1: Overland Park → Des Moines (~190 mi, ~3h)

I-435 N → I-35 N straight through Bethany and into Iowa. Flat, fast, mostly featureless — this is the "make miles" portion. Des Moines is a reasonable lunch stop or leg-stretch; the East Village near the gold-domed Iowa Capitol has decent coffee/food if you want to make it slightly more than a gas stop.

## Segment 2: Des Moines → Cedar Rapids → Dubuque (~190 mi, ~3h)

Continue I-35 N briefly, then **I-80 E** across central Iowa through Iowa City, then **US-151 N** out of Cedar Rapids toward Dubuque. 
Major cities along the route include Cedar Rapids, IA, and Des Moines, IA.
 The landscape gets more interesting after Iowa City — rolling hills heading into the Driftless Area.

Dubuque on the Mississippi is the natural overnight or long-stop candidate if splitting the drive. 
Dubuque, situated along the Mississippi River, offers the National Mississippi River Museum and Aquarium, which provides an immersive experience into the river's ecosystem.
 Eagle Point Park has river overlooks if you want a 30-minute leg-stretch with actual views.

## Segment 3: Dubuque → Madison (~95 mi, ~1h 45m)

Cross the Mississippi on US-20, optionally detour 15 minutes south to **Galena, IL** (well worth it — see Stops). Then **US-151 N** through the Driftless rolling hills into Madison. 
The direct drive from Galena to Madison is 95.1 mi (153 km), and should have a drive time of 1 hr 55 mins in normal traffic.


This is the prettiest segment of the whole route — rolling farmland, river valleys, small towns. If time allows, the New Glarus detour (~30 min south of Madison via WI-69) is the quintessential Wisconsin drive: 
35 mins on country roads cutting through corn fields — quintessential Wisconsin scenery of rolling hills
. New Glarus Brewing's beer is famously not distributed outside Wisconsin.

## Arrival in Madison

US-151 turns into the Beltline (US-12/18) on Madison's south side. Take the **John Nolen Drive** exit north — this is the postcard approach, threading between Lake Monona and Lake Wingra straight at Monona Terrace and the Capitol dome. Don't take the Beltline-to-downtown shortcuts; John Nolen is the move.

Parking downtown: street parking around Capitol Square is metered but plentiful evenings/Sundays. 
Park in the State Street Capitol Garage
 for Saturday market access (downtown street closures kick in around 8 AM Saturday). 
West Mifflin Street, State Street, and North Carroll Street close to motor vehicle traffic each Saturday from approximately 8:00am until 2:00pm.


## Return route options

**Direct return:** reverse the route, ~7.5 hrs. Doable in a day if leaving Madison by 9 AM.

**Variant — split through Galena:** overnight Sunday in Galena (or Dubuque), drive home Monday. Galena's 19th-century downtown is a real destination on its own and turns the return into part of the trip rather than just transit. Trade-off: an extra night of cat-sitter cost.
